# Social Engineering

## What is social engineering?

Social engineering, in the context of information security, refers to psychological manipulation of people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. This differs from social engineering within the social sciences, which does not contain the divulging of confidential information.

## Social Engineering - On Site with Employee

On Site Phishing refers to the act of social engineering the company staff at the physical office location. This usually includes impersonation that is pretending or pretexting to be another person with the goal of gaining access physically to a system or building.

## Social Engineering – Fake Wifi captive portal

A Fake Wifi Captive portal spawns a new SSID with a name similar or same as the actually company SSID with the intent of making users connect to it and enter their credentials on the captive page.

## Social Engineering – Email phishing activity

Email Phishing is a type of social engineering attack often used to steal user data, including login credentials and credit card numbers. It occurs when an attacker, masquerading as a trusted entity, dupes a victim into opening an email, instant message, or text message. The recipient is then tricked into clicking a malicious link, which can lead to the installation of malware, the freezing of the system as part of a ransomware attack or the revealing of sensitive information.

## Physical Security - Attempt to clone RFID Card

Most employee ID cards have RFID capabilities which enable them to check in / out , open doors secured by RFID Readers. Getting access to such card enables easy physical access to all locations the card had access to. RFID cloning is the process of cloning the data by an attacker from a legitimate card to a blank card, without the knowledge of employee. The cloned card will act the same as the original card. This is a huge physical security risk since it allows unauthorized physical access to the location.

## Phishing via Call \(Vishing\)

Vishing refers to the phishing activity via a call phone. This method basically refers to impersonation over phone. This involves calling a target pretending to be someone with authority. Before making the call from a new number, changing the truecaller entry might help. Once the target is convinced we can extract the relevant information or coerce the target into committing an action of our choice.
